# Research themes
In the fields of natural language processing and computational linguistics, our research centers on the question, “What are we doing—and what can we learn—by modeling linguistic intelligence?” We conduct our research while placing great importance on perspectives from linguistics, psychology, and philosophy. Currently, we are focusing on the following three main topics:

## 1. Evaluation of Linguistic Intelligence and Its Methodology
We are working on constructing datasets and improving methodologies for evaluating the behavior of language models. In particular, we aim to enhance the reliability of language model evaluations by applying concepts of measurement validity from (psychometric) psychology. 

Related Fields: Psychometrics (Psychological Measurement), Education

## 2. Language Acquisition and Processing in Humans and Language Models
We explore the principles underlying the acquisition and processing of linguistic behavior in humans and language models through a comparative analysis of their language acquisition and processing. In particular, we are interested in the question of how acquisition and processing that more closely resemble human behavior can be realized in language models

Related Fields: Language Acquisition, Psycholinguistics, Cognitive Science

### Examples of past research results

- Haga et al. (ACL 2024 Findings) Modeling Overregularization in Children with Small Language Models
- Asami and Sugawara (ACL 2024 Findings) What Makes Language Models Good-enough?

## 3. Philosophy of Computational Linguistics
We explore what we are doing and what we can know through language model research, considering epistemological and ontological definitions of intelligence and language understanding, as well as perspectives such as scientific models in the philosophy of science. At the same time, we examine connections with constructivist concepts in psychology.

Related Fields: Philosophy of Science, Philosophy of Language, Epistemology
